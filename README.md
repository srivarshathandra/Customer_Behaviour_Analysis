# Customer_Behaviour_Analysis📈🚀
Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis
A comprehensive data analytics project analyzing customer purchasing patterns, demographic insights, and business trends from a dataset of 3,900 retail transactions.

# 📋 Project Overview
This project uncovers actionable insights into customer spending patterns, product preferences, subscription behavior, and demographic segments. The analysis encompasses exploratory data analysis (EDA) in Python, structured SQL queries in PostgreSQL, interactive visualization in Power BI, and professional reporting.
Goal: Guide strategic business decisions through data-driven recommendations on customer retention, product positioning, and targeted marketing.

## 📊 Dataset Summary

Records : 3,900 customer transactions
Columns : 18 features covering demographics, purchase details, and behavioral metrics
Missing Data: 37 values in Review Rating column (imputed using category medians)

Key Features
Customer Demographics:Age, Gender, Location,Subscription Status,Customer ID

Purchase Details: Item Purchased & Product Category,Purchase Amount (USD),Season, Size, Color preferences

Shopping Behavior: Discount Applied,Previous Purchase Count,Purchase Frequency,Review Rating,Shipping Type (Standard/Express)


### 🛠 Tools & Technologies

# 💡 Business Recommendations
1. Boost Subscriptions

Action: Promote exclusive benefits for subscribers (early access, loyalty points, discounts on future purchases)
Rationale: Subscribers spend slightly less per transaction but provide predictable recurring revenue. Increasing subscription adoption is critical for revenue stability.
Target: Convert the 73% non-subscriber base through tiered subscription tiers.

2. Customer Loyalty Programs

Action: Implement rewards program targeting repeat buyers
Rationale: 3,116 loyal customers (80% of base) are retention goldmines. Move returning customers (701) into the loyal segment with targeted incentives.
Target Incentive: Points per purchase, tiered rewards tiers, exclusive perks at 5+ purchases.

3. Optimize Discount Strategy

Action: Balance margin protection with discount dependency of specific products
Rationale: Hat, Sneakers, and Coat are 50%+ discount-dependent. Reduce reliance through:

Bundle deals (pairing discount-dependent items with full-price complementary products)
Limited-time flash sales instead of always-on discounts
Quality differentiation for premium variants


Target: Reduce discount dependency from 50% to 35% within 6 months.

4. Product Positioning

Action: Highlight top-rated and best-selling products in marketing campaigns
Rationale: Gloves (3.86), Sandals (3.84), and Boots (3.82) have highest ratings. Jewelry, Blouse, and Jacket are best-sellers.
Campaigns: Feature these products prominently in email, social, and point-of-sale.

5. Targeted Marketing by Demographic

Action: Tailor campaigns by age group and purchase behavior
Rationale:

Young Adults drive 11% more revenue than Seniors—emphasize trendy, fashion-forward items
Express shipping users (higher spend) may respond to premium/convenience messaging
Female customers underperform in revenue—test feminine-focused product lines or campaigns


Channel: Age-specific social media, geo-targeted ads, email segmentation.

6. New & Returning Customer Acquisition

Action: Develop onboarding and re-engagement campaigns for the 784 new/returning customers
Rationale: Convert new customers (83) to repeat buyers and reactivate returning customers (701).
Initiatives:

Welcome discount (first purchase incentive)
30-day re-engagement email for returning customers
Personalized product recommendations based on category affinity
